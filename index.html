Our Intelligence Engines

Six Proprietary ZATO Modules

ZATO-AeroGuard's architecture comprises six specialised intelligence modules each precision-engineered to transform aviation technical records from a compliance burden into a primary driver of asset liquidity and financial certainty.

Engine A

Transactional Digital Twin Engine

Creates a living virtual replica of the aircraft's technical and legal status...

Using Deep Convolutional Neural Networks and LayoutLM-based document understanding, the Transactional Digital Twin builds a continuously updated virtual replica of each aircraft's full technical, legal, and financial status. Every maintenance event, LLP trace, SB/AD compliance record, and structural repair is mapped in real-time enabling lessors and CAMOs to monitor asset airworthiness and redelivery readiness from a single intelligence dashboard, with sub-200ms data retrieval latency.

Engine B

Asset Stability Score (ASS)

The industry's first quantitative metric...

ZATO's proprietary scoring engine analyses over 40 weighted parameters including documentation completeness, AD/SB compliance status, LLP life remaining, historical repair quality, and cross-border re-registration risk to produce a single Asset Stability Score (0–100). This score gives lessors, investors, and banks an objective, auditable measure of an aircraft's financial liquidity and airworthiness confidence at any point in the asset lifecycle.

Engine C

Regulatory Friction Index (RFI)

Automated compliance mapper...

ZATO's RFI module cross-references live regulatory databases across UK CAA, EASA, FAA, TCCA, and CAAC to compute the precise documentation gaps, estimated re-registration timelines, and associated cost exposure for any cross-border aircraft migration. Lessors receive an automated friction report quantifying expected delays, outstanding compliance actions, and financial risk exposure weeks before a transition event occurs, enabling proactive mitigation.

Engine D

Blockchain Handover Ledger

A private blockchain trust layer...

ZATO employs a private, permissioned blockchain ledger to create an immutable Chain of Evidence for every aircraft transition event. Technical sign-offs, document uploads, airworthiness verifications, and CAA/EASA submissions are cryptographically timestamped and stored eliminating "missing record" disputes between lessors and lessees, and providing aviation banks and legal advisors with a legally defensible audit trail from day one of asset ownership transfer.

Engine E

Hangar Edge AI Hubs

Pre-configured physical edge-AI units...

ZATO's Hangar Edge Hubs are pre-configured physical AI processing units deployed directly at MRO facilities and hangar sites enabling offline-capable document ingestion, real-time OCR processing, and secure data synchronisation even in low-connectivity hangar environments. Each hub integrates with AMOS, TRAX, and Ultramain via RESTful APIs, ensuring zero-gap data continuity between line maintenance operations and the central Digital Twin platform.

Engine F

Scenario-Based Transition Simulation

Months before lease expiry...

The simulation engine incorporates fleet-wide historical transition data, MRO cost benchmarks, and current regulatory timelines to model multiple lease-end scenarios months before expiry. Lessors can stress-test documentation readiness, estimate redelivery compensation exposure, and optimise transition sequencing across multi-asset portfolios converting reactive audit firefighting into a proactive financial planning discipline with measurable ROI at each transition milestone.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Transactional Digital Twin and how is it different from a passive records system?

+
A Transactional Digital Twin is a living, real-time virtual replica of an aircraft's technical and legal status updated continuously as maintenance events, component swaps, and regulatory changes occur. Unlike passive record hubs (FLYdocs, AirVault) that simply store and digitise documents, ZATO-AeroGuard actively analyses the records, identifies compliance "clashes," computes financial liability, and predicts audit failures months before a transition event functioning as an Active Intelligence layer rather than a static archive.

How does ZATO handle handwritten "dirty fingerprint" maintenance logs?

+
ZATO-AeroGuard employs a proprietary AI OCR engine built on LayoutLM and DenseNet architectures, specifically fine-tuned on legacy aviation handwritten technical records including maintenance release certificates, structural repair maps, and BTB trace documents. Testing has confirmed 94% extraction accuracy on legacy handwritten archives a core technical barrier that generic OCR solutions cannot replicate.

Is ZATO compliant with UK CAA CAP 1330 and international airworthiness standards?

+
Yes. ZATO-AeroGuard is the first aviation records platform specifically engineered to meet the UK Civil Aviation Authority's Digital Technical Records mandate (CAP 1330). The platform is also aligned with EASA Part-M/Part-CAMO requirements, ATA Spec 2500, ISO/IEC 27001, GDPR, and Cyber Essentials Plus. Compliance documentation and DPIAs are maintained under a version-controlled Quality Management System.

What is the Regulatory Friction Index (RFI) and why does it matter?

+
The Regulatory Friction Index is ZATO's automated compliance mapper that computes the estimated time and cost required to migrate an aircraft between global regulatory registries (UK CAA, EASA, FAA). Without such a tool, lessors frequently underestimate re-registration complexity leading to missed delivery windows and contractual penalties that can reach six figures per week.

Can ZATO integrate with existing MRO software such as AMOS and TRAX?

+
Yes. ZATO-AeroGuard's integration layer utilises RESTful APIs that support direct data ingestion from major aviation ERP systems including AMOS, TRAX, and Ultramain. Apache Airflow and Kafka pipelines automate the ingestion of technical record packages providing a unified transactional view across all source systems without requiring legacy system replacement.
